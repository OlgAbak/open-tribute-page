Margaret Thatcher

The woman who reshaped domestic and global politics

Here's a time line of Margaret Thatcher's life:

  • 1925 - Born at Grantham, eastern England
  • 1943 - 1947 - Studies chemistry at Oxford's Somerville College
  • 1950 - Runs as the Conservative candidate for the strong Labour seat of Dartford at the General Elections
  • 1951 - Marries Denis Thatcher, a Dartford businessman
  • 1953 - Gives birth to twins - Mark and Carol
  • 1959 - Elected to Parliament as Member of Parliament for Finchley, a north London constituency
  • 1961 - Given junior office in the administration of Harold Macmillan
  • 1964 - Establishes her place among the senior figures of the Conservative Party serving continuously as a shadow minister
  • 1970 - Achieves cabinet rank as Education Secretary
  • 1975 - Defeats Heath, the then leader of the Conservatives, on the first ballot and wins the contest outright on the second, though challenged by half a dozen senior colleagues
  • 1979 - Becomes Prime Minister of the United Kindom the day after the Conservatives won a Parliamentary majority of 43 at the General Election
  • 1981 - Britain's economic recovery starts as a result of painful economic measures taken by her government
  • 1982 - Wins the Falklands War resulting in the Falklands being back under British control
  • 1983 - Re-elected with her government's Parlamentary majority more than trebled (144 seats)
  • 1984 - Bombed by the Irish Republican Army (IRA) in her hotel in Brighton during the Conservative Party annual conference, survives unhurt, no twentieth-century British Prime Minister ever came closer to assassination
  • 1985 - Negotiates the Anglo-Irish Agreement with the Republic of Ireland to improve security cooperation between Britain and Ireland and to give some recognition to the political outlook of Catholics in Northern Ireland
  • 1986 - Subjected to heavy criticism within her own party for the decision to allow US warplanes to fly from British bases to attack targets in Libya
  • 1987 - Re-elected with her government's Parlamentary majority of 101 seats
  • 1988 - Takes measures to reform the British education system, introducing a national curriculum for the first time
  • 1989 - Introduces a new tax system for local government
  • 1990 - Introduces legislation to separate purchasers and providers within the National Health Service, opening up the service to a measure of competition for the first time and increasing the scope for effective management
  • 1990 - Resignes as Prime Minister as a result of acute divisions over European policy within the British Government by the end of the Cold War
  • 1993 - Writes best-selling memoirs - The Downing Street Years and The Path to Power
  • 2002 - Publishes a book of reflections on international politics - Statecraft
  • 2002 - Announces an end to her career in public speaking
  • 2013 - Dies in London, honoured at a ceremonial funeral in St Paul's Cathedral
